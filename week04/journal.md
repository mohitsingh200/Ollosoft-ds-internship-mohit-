# Week 4 Journal

## Day 1 - Descriptive Statistics

Today I learned descriptive statistics for financial data. I calculated mean, median, standard deviation, minimum, maximum and quartiles for stock returns. I explored how these measures help summarize the behavior of stock prices.



## Day 2 - Probability Distributions

Today I studied probability distributions using stock returns. I created histograms, fitted a normal distribution and generated a Q-Q plot. I observed that stock returns are not perfectly normally distributed and can contain extreme values.



## Day 3 - Hypothesis Testing

Today I learned the basics of hypothesis testing. I performed statistical tests on stock return data and practiced interpreting p-values. I understood how hypothesis testing can be used to evaluate financial assumptions.



## Day 4 - Correlation and Covariance

Today I calculated correlation and covariance among five Indian large-cap stocks. I created a correlation heatmap and identified highly correlated and less correlated stock pairs. I learned how diversification can reduce portfolio risk.


## Day 5 - Returns and Volatility

Today I calculated log returns, annualized returns and annualized volatility. I computed Sharpe Ratios using a risk-free rate of 6.5% and ranked the stocks based on risk-adjusted performance. I also visualized the results using a bar chart.



## Overall Week 4 Reflection

This week helped me understand the statistical foundations of financial analysis. I learned how to analyze stock return distributions, measure relationships between stocks and evaluate performance using risk-adjusted metrics. I became more comfortable using Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn and SciPy for financial data analysis.
